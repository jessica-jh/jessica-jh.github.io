Jihye (Jessica) Kim

Ph.D., KAIST · M.S. Student, UC Santa Cruz
Causal Inference & Econometrics NLP & LLM Research

I am a researcher applying econometric and causal inference methods to study LLM safety, trustworthiness, and alignment. I earned my Ph.D. in Information Systems at KAIST (Dept. of Management Engineering), where I studied the societal and market impacts of technology-enabled interventions—from fintech adoption to automation—using large-scale observational and experimental data (published in Information Systems Research, UTD24/FT50). Now, as an M.S. student in NLP at University of California, Santa Cruz (Dept. of Computer Science and Engineering), I study the deceptive behaviors of LLM agents and their vulnerability to external manipulation—designing structural interventions to mitigate these risks. Prior to academia, I spent 3+ years as a Senior Consultant at Deloitte Consulting, leading digital transformation and performance management projects across multiple industries.

Research

Two tracks, one question: how do we build reliable evidence about complex systems?

Track I

Causal Inference & Econometrics

Published: Information Systems Research (UTD24/FT50)
In Revision: MSOM (UTD24/FT50) · Resubmission: JMIS
Accepted: CIST '22, '23 · WISE '24 · HICSS '24 · Marketing Science DEI '23 · KrAIS '23

I design causal identification strategies to measure the real-world effects of technology-enabled interventions on economic behavior—from on-demand wage access changing financial habits of low-wage workers, to self-order kiosks reshaping consumer demand patterns.

Staggered DID PSM / IPTW IV Double ML Causal Forest A/B Testing
Track II

NLP & LLM Research

Accepted: ACL 2026 TrustNLP · SemEval 2026
In Progress: Verbal-probabilistic alignment in LLMs

I study how large language models behave in high-stakes interactive settings—negotiation, persuasion, decision-making—and how structural interventions can improve their reliability. My work uncovered "Deceptive Bypass" in LLM agents and proposed observer–planner–actor architectures to mitigate it.

LLM Agents Trustworthy AI Prompt Engineering RAG Fine-tuning Alignment
Both tracks share a core methodology: careful identification of what causes what—whether the "treatment" is a fintech product or a prompt architecture, and whether the "subject" is a worker or a language model.

Information Systems Research (ISR) · UTD24/FT50 · 2026
Jihye Kim*, Seokchae Yoon, Sunghun Chung, Wonseok Oh
Estimated causal effects of on-demand wage access on 4,000 low-wage workers using staggered DID, PSM, IV, and Double ML; quantified +12.9% monitoring duration and +3.7% saving frequency. Validated via randomized experiment (Prolific).
